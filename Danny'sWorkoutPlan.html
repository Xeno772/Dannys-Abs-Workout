Welcome to Danny's Fitness Journey!

This plan is designed by me (aka the goat) to help you get a six pack as soon as possible!


Important Things to Remember:

  • Warm-up: Start with 5-10 minutes of light cardio like jogging in place, jumping jacks, and dynamic stretching (arm circles, leg swings).
  • Hurdling: Include 10 minutes of light hurdle exercises or leg swings to improve flexibility.
  • Stay Confident: Believe in yourself and your ability to improve!
  • Proper Form: Focus on doing each exercise correctly to avoid injury. If you're not sure how to do it, ask a parent, coach, or watch reliable videos.
  • Be Patient: You might not see big changes right away, but stick with it and you will get stronger!
  • Listen to Your Body: If something feels painful, stop and rest. Don't push yourself too hard, especially when you're starting.
  • Adjust as Needed: This is just a plan, feel free to make small changes if an exercise doesn't feel right or if you want to try something similar.
  • Cool-down: Finish each workout with 5-10 minutes of static stretching (hold each stretch for 20-30 seconds).
  • Nutrition: Eat a balanced diet with plenty of fruits, vegetables, protein, and whole grains. Drink plenty of water throughout the day.
  • Rest: Get 8-10 hours of sleep each night to allow your body to recover and grow.

Monday: Core Power

Focus on Your Abs and Core:

Crunches: 3 sets of 15-20 reps. Lie on your back with knees bent and feet flat. Lift your shoulders off the ground, squeezing your abs.

Leg Raises: 3 sets of 15-20 reps. Lie on your back with legs straight. Slowly lift your legs towards the ceiling, keeping them straight or slightly bent. Lower them slowly.

Bicycle Crunches: 3 sets of 15-20 reps per side. Lie on your back with hands behind your head. Bring one knee towards your chest while twisting your opposite elbow towards that knee. Alternate sides.

Plank: 3 sets, hold for 30-60 seconds. Hold a push-up position with your forearms on the ground. Keep your body in a straight line from head to heels.

Russian Twists (no weight needed to start): 3 sets of 15-20 reps per side. Sit with your knees bent and feet slightly off the ground. Twist your upper body from side to side, keeping your back straight.

Tuesday: Full Body Fun

Working All Your Muscles:

Squats: 3 sets of 10-12 reps. Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart. Lower your hips as if you're sitting in a chair, keeping your back straight and knees behind your toes.

Push-ups: 3 sets to as many reps as you can do with good form. Start in a plank position and lower your chest to the ground by bending your elbows. Push back up. If they're too hard, try doing them on your knees.

Rows (using a resistance band or light dumbbells if available): 3 sets of 10-12 reps per arm. Stand with feet hip-width apart, hinge slightly at your hips, and pull the band or dumbbells towards your chest, squeezing your back muscles.

Overhead Press (using light dumbbells if available): 3 sets of 10-12 reps. Stand with feet hip-width apart, holding a dumbbell in each hand at shoulder height. Press the dumbbells overhead, extending your arms fully. Lower back to shoulder height.

Jumping Jacks: 3 sets of 20-25 reps. Stand with feet together and arms at your sides. Jump, spreading your feet and raising your arms overhead. Return to the starting position and repeat.

Wednesday: Break Day

Today is a rest day. Focus on active recovery, such as light stretching, walking, or other low-intensity activities. This will help your muscles recover and prevent soreness. Make sure to get plenty of sleep and eat nutritious meals.

Thursday: Cardio and Core

Elevate your heart rate and strengthen your core

Jogging/Running: 20-30 minutes of moderate-intensity running or jogging. If you're new to running, start with shorter intervals (e.g., 5 minutes running, 2 minutes walking) and gradually increase the running time.

High Knees: 3 sets of 20-30 seconds. Stand in place and lift each knee towards your chest, alternating legs quickly.

Butt Kicks: 3 sets of 20-30 seconds. Stand in place and kick your heels towards your glutes, alternating legs quickly.

Plank: 3 sets, hold for 30-60 seconds. Maintain a straight line from head to heels, engaging your core muscles.

Side Plank (each side): 3 sets, hold for 30-60 seconds per side. Lie on your side and lift your hips off the ground, supporting your weight on your forearm. Keep your body in a straight line.

Crunches: 3 sets of 15-20 reps.

Friday: Break Day

Today is a rest day. Focus on active recovery, such as light stretching, walking, or other low-intensity activities. This will help your muscles recover and prevent soreness. Make sure to get plenty of sleep and eat nutritious meals.

Saturday: Strength Training

Build overall strength

Lunges: 3 sets of 10-12 reps per leg. Step forward with one leg and lower your body until both knees are bent at about 90 degrees. Keep your front knee behind your toes.

Push-ups: 3 sets to as many reps as possible with good form.

Glute Bridges: 3 sets of 15-20 reps. Lie on your back with knees bent and feet flat. Lift your hips off the ground, squeezing your glutes.

Superman: 3 sets of 15-20 reps. Lie on your stomach with arms and legs extended. Lift your arms, chest, and legs off the ground simultaneously.

Plank: 3 sets, hold for 30-60 seconds.

Sunday: Break Day

Today is a rest day. Enjoy your day off! You can do some light stretching or other very light activity if you feel up to it, but the main goal is to rest and recover. Focus on getting good sleep and eating well.

Extra Idea: Sports and Activities

Besides this plan, try to be active in other ways! Join a sports team, go for bike rides, swim, dance, or just play outside with friends. The most important thing is to find activities you enjoy so you'll stick with them.
